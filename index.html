The MLB
AI Visibility Index

With the rise of AI, the way people discover, evaluate, and talk about MLB teams is changing. Fans, travelers, sponsors, media, and even future players are asking AI which teams matter, which ballparks are worth visiting, which clubs are best run, and which organizations give sponsors the strongest platform.

The engines are already answering.

The MLB AI Visibility Index measures which teams show up in those answers, how often they appear, and whether winning, payroll, market size, or brand strength actually translates into AI visibility.

Methodology

What This Index Actually Measures

AI visibility is not a ranking of the best teams in baseball. It is a measure of how often AI engines surface each club when asked the kinds of questions that shape perception, attention, and interest.

We tested 100 team-neutral questions across eight categories: fan experience, talent and organizational reputation, audience and reach, sponsorship, market relevance, business and franchise strength, brand identity, and a new-fan / who-to-follow category.

Each question was run through five major AI answer engines: ChatGPT, Claude, Google Gemini, Perplexity, and Google AI Overview. The search engines were each tested three times per question, while Google AI Overview was captured once per question, generating 1,300 answers in total.

Every team mention was counted and scored based on how prominently the club appeared.

The goal was simple: understand which MLB teams AI consistently highlights, which teams it overlooks, and where the results challenge what you would expect from payroll, standings, market size, or real-world popularity.

Because whether teams are optimizing for it or not, AI is already becoming a new layer of sports discovery. This report shows who is winning attention there.

How we counted

100 team-neutral prompts across 8 categories. ChatGPT (GPT-5.5), Claude (Opus 4.8), Gemini 2.5 Pro, and Perplexity (Sonar) each answered all 100 questions three times with web search enabled; Google's AI Overview was captured once via SerpApi. Captured late May 2026, US/English — 1,300 responses and 7,772 team mentions in all. These five cover the large majority of AI answer traffic.

We set the scoring rules before reading any answers. First team named = 5 pts, second = 3, any other mention = 1, ballpark named without the team = 1 (one score per team per answer). Summed, then set the leader to 100 — that's the AI visibility score. Category scores count mentions within that category only.

When an answer named a ballpark but not the team (e.g. Wrigley), we credited the team — every such case is logged.

What it is (and isn't)

These are the engines' answers, not ours — we only counted. It measures how often AI surfaces a team, not whether the team is good. Models drift, so we refresh it.
